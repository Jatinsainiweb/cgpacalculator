What is CGPA?

CGPA (Cumulative Grade Point Average) is a standardized method to measure a student’s overall academic performance across all semesters. It is widely used in Indian universities and schools to provide a summary of a student’s grades.

How to Calculate CGPA – Step-by-Step

  1. List all your subjects and the grade points earned in each subject.
  2. Add up all the grade points.
  3. Count the total number of subjects.
  4. Divide the total grade points by the number of subjects.
  5. The result is your CGPA.

CGPA Calculation Formula

The general formula for calculating CGPA is:

CGPA = (Sum of Grade Points in all subjects) / (Total Number of Subjects)

Some universities may use credit-weighted averages. Always check your university’s guidelines.

CGPA Calculation for Credit-Based Systems

Some universities use credits for each subject. The formula becomes:

CGPA = (Sum of (Grade Point × Credit) for all subjects) / (Total Credits)

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. What is CGPA?

CGPA stands for Cumulative Grade Point Average. It summarizes your academic performance across all subjects or semesters.

2. How do I calculate CGPA if my university uses credits?

Multiply each subject’s grade point by its credit, sum all, and divide by total credits.

3. Can I use decimal grade points?

Yes, decimal grade points (e.g., 8.25) are common and accepted.

4. Is CGPA the same as percentage?

No, CGPA is a grade point average. To convert to percentage, use your university’s conversion formula (e.g., CGPA × 9.5).
