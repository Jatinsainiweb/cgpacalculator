Introduction

Understanding CGPA

CGPA (Cumulative Grade Point Average) is a 10-point grading system used by Indian universities like CBSE, VTU, and Anna University to evaluate overall academic performance. It averages your grades across all semesters, providing a consolidated score. For instance, a 9.0 CGPA reflects consistent excellence.

Why Convert CGPA to Percentage?

Percentages are crucial for:

  • Job Applications: Companies like Infosys and Wipro often require a minimum of 60% for eligibility.
  • Higher Education Abroad: Universities in the USA, UK, or Australia need percentage equivalents for admissions.
  • Government Exams: UPSC, SSC, and state-level exams mandate percentage-based criteria.

Example:

A CBSE student with 8.5 CGPA converts it to percentage as:

8.5 × 9.5 = 80.75%

Formula Variations Across Institutions

Standard CBSE Formula

CBSE's widely accepted formula is:

Percentage = CGPA × 9.5

This multiplier (9.5) aligns the 10-point CGPA scale with the traditional 95% maximum percentage.

University-Specific Multipliers
