Understanding SGPA and CGPA

SGPA (Semester Grade Point Average) represents your academic performance in a single semester, while CGPA (Cumulative Grade Point Average) represents your overall academic performance across all semesters.

How CGPA is Calculated from SGPA

CGPA = (SGPA₁ + SGPA₂ + SGPA₃ + ... + SGPAₙ) ÷ n

Where n = number of semesters

Example Calculation

For a student with 4 semesters:

  • Semester 1 SGPA: 8.5
  • Semester 2 SGPA: 9.0
  • Semester 3 SGPA: 8.7
  • Semester 4 SGPA: 9.2

CGPA Calculation:

CGPA = (8.5 + 9.0 + 8.7 + 9.2) ÷ 4

CGPA = 35.4 ÷ 4 = 8.85

Important Notes

  • SGPA is calculated per semester
  • CGPA considers all semesters equally
  • Some universities may use weighted averages
  • Keep track of all semester SGPAs
  • Most universities use a 10-point scale

Common Conversions

  • CGPA to Percentage = CGPA × 9.5 (Most universities)
  • CGPA to Percentage = CGPA × 10 (Some universities)
  • 4.0 Scale = (Indian CGPA × 4) ÷ 10
