What is CGPA?

CGPA (Cumulative Grade Point Average) is a standardized academic performance metric widely used by universities worldwide. It provides a comprehensive evaluation of a student's academic journey by averaging grade points across all semesters. Unlike percentage systems, CGPA offers a more nuanced and standardized way to assess academic achievement, making it easier to compare performance across different educational institutions.

How to Calculate CGPA (With Example)

Understanding how to calculate CGPA is crucial for every student. Let's break down the process with a practical example:

Step-by-Step CGPA Calculation:

  1. 1. Multiply each course's grade point by its credit hours
  2. 2. Sum up all these products to get total grade points
  3. 3. Add up all credit hours attempted
  4. 4. Divide total grade points by total credit hours

Example Calculation:

Subject Credits Grade Points Total Points
Mathematics 4 9 36
Physics 3 8 24
Chemistry 3 7 21

Total Points: 81
Total Credits: 10
CGPA = 81 ÷ 10 = 8.1

CGPA to Percentage Formula

Converting CGPA to percentage is essential for various academic and professional purposes. The standard formula used by most Indian universities is:

Percentage = CGPA × 9.5

For example, if your CGPA is 8.5:

Percentage = 8.5 × 9.5 = 80.75%

Percentage to CGPA Formula

To convert your percentage back to CGPA, use this formula:

CGPA = Percentage ÷ 9.5

For example, if your percentage is 75%:

CGPA = 75 ÷ 9.5 = 7.89

SGPA to CGPA Conversion

Converting SGPA (Semester Grade Point Average) to CGPA requires considering the credits for each semester. The formula is:

CGPA = (SGPA₁ × Credits₁ + SGPA₂ × Credits₂ + ...) ÷ Total Credits

Example:

Semester 1: SGPA = 8.2, Credits = 20
Semester 2: SGPA = 8.6, Credits = 22

CGPA = (8.2 × 20 + 8.6 × 22) ÷ (20 + 22)
= (164 + 189.2) ÷ 42
= 353.2 ÷ 42
= 8.41
