What is SGPA?

The Semester Grade Point Average (SGPA) is a crucial measure used by educational institutions to reflect a student's academic performance in a single semester. Unlike the cumulative grade point average (CGPA), which aggregates results from multiple semesters, SGPA focuses on one specific term or semester.

It helps you:

  • Assess your academic progress
  • Understand how well you performed in each subject
  • Identify areas to improve
  • Calculate your overall CGPA at the end of your course

How Does SGPA Work?

SGPA combines your subject grades and credit hours into a single weighted average. Every course or subject you take has a specific credit value based on the number of hours or importance assigned by your university. Along with that, each grade corresponds to a grade point (usually between 0 and 10).

The SGPA is computed by multiplying each subject's grade point by its credit value, summing all these products, and then dividing by the total number of credits taken in that semester.

Formula to Calculate SGPA

SGPA = Σ(Grade Point × Credit) ÷ Σ(Credits)

Example Calculation

Imagine you took the following courses this semester:

Subject Credits Grade Grade Point
Physics 3 A 9
Mathematics 4 B+ 8.5
Chemistry 3 A+ 10
English 2 B 8

Multiply each grade point by its credit:

  • Physics: 3 × 9 = 27
  • Mathematics: 4 × 8.5 = 34
  • Chemistry: 3 × 10 = 30
  • English: 2 × 8 = 16

Sum of (grade point × credits) = 27 + 34 + 30 + 16 = 107
Sum of credits = 3 + 4 + 3 + 2 = 12

Now, calculate SGPA:

SGPA = 107 ÷ 12 = 8.92

Your SGPA for this semester is 8.92.

Why SGPA is Important

Your SGPA gives you a clear snapshot of your semester performance and helps track your academic journey in real-time. Universities often use SGPA to:

  • Calculate the CGPA (cumulative average across semesters)
  • Decide eligibility for scholarships or academic awards
  • Determine eligibility for placements and internships
  • Analyze trends and academic progress over semesters

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Q1: What's the difference between SGPA and CGPA?

SGPA is semester-specific, showing your performance in a single semester, while CGPA is the average of all your semesters combined.

Q2: Can I use this calculator if my university has a different grading scale?

Yes. You can customize credits and grade points to match your university's system.

Q3: How can I convert my SGPA to percentage?

Most universities provide conversion formulas. A common one is:
Percentage = (SGPA - 0.75) × 10
Please verify with your institution.

Q4: Will this calculator help if I have backlogs?

Yes, but failed subjects typically have grade points as 0, which will reduce your overall SGPA.

Q5: Is this calculator mobile-friendly?

Yes, you can use this SGPA calculator on any device—desktop, tablet, or mobile.
