Understanding CGPA Calculation

CGPA (Cumulative Grade Point Average) is calculated using the following formula:

CGPA = Σ(Course Credits × Grade Points) / Σ(Total Credits)

Where:

  • Course Credits = Number of credits assigned to the course
  • Grade Points = Points assigned for the grade obtained (usually on a 10-point scale)
  • Total Credits = Sum of credits of all courses

Step-by-Step Guide

  4. Add additional semesters as needed

Standard Grade Points (10-Point Scale)

  • O / A+ (Outstanding) = 10 points
  • A (Excellent) = 9 points
  • B+ (Very Good) = 8 points
  • B (Good) = 7 points
  • C+ (Above Average) = 6 points
  • C (Average) = 5 points
  • D (Pass) = 4 points
  • F (Fail) = 0 points

Important Notes

  • Credits vary by course importance
  • Theory courses: Usually 3-4 credits
  • Lab courses: Usually 1-2 credits
  • Project work: Usually 4-6 credits

Example Calculation

For a semester with 3 courses:

  • Course 1: 4 credits, Grade A (9 points)
  • Course 2: 3 credits, Grade B+ (8 points)
  • Course 3: 2 credits, Grade A+ (10 points)
  • Total Credits = 9
  • Total Points = (4×9) + (3×8) + (2×10) = 36 + 24 + 20 = 80
  • CGPA = 80/9 = 8.89
